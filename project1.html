Food Service Project



Introduction

This was a student project that was a part of my introduction to user centered design class in my master’s program. It focused on user experience issues in the food service industry, specifically those faced by employees in quick service restaurants.

Problem Statement

Employees in quick service restaurants are in a position to benefit immensely from user experience because of the environment that they work in. The environment is fast-paced and interruption heavy, while still requiring that employees complete tasks in a satisfactory amount of time for the customer. With this in mind, my team designed a different quick service POS system with features that would support employees by reducing cognitive load.

My Role

My role in this project was to plan out and conduct pieces of the preliminary research, write process overviews, contribute to ideation and prototyping with my own sketched designs, and conduct some basic usability testing to find ways to improve the design.

Methodology

Our methodology included preliminary research in the form of observation and semi-structured interviews. We then conducted a work activity affinity diagram to help us identify problems and make design deliverables. We then conducted usability testing by giving participants tasks to complete and following up with another semi-structured interview.

Findings and Results

Our basic findings from preliminary interviews with employees in the domain were that employees in quick service environments struggle with high cognitive load and being interrupted by both managers and customers while trying to complete tasks efficiently. These findings led to unique features that we added to our design, including a recipes tab, for quick service employees to use when they are interrupted and don’t remember exactly which step they left off on. We also implemented a reminder system that would make use of popups on screens to remind employees of certain tasks or orders after an allotted amount of time had passed without action being taken, such as an order being submitted to the system or being marked as complete.

What I Learned

I came away from this project having learned a lot about the design process as a whole. I had never gone through the entire process with one project before and being able to do that was very valuable, as it allowed me to see how one part of the process flows into another. I also came away with a better understanding of how team-based design works and how time limits can prevent iteration and improvement. If I had more time on this project, I would have made refinements based on the usability testing we conducted and iterated upon those tests with more context and better design.
